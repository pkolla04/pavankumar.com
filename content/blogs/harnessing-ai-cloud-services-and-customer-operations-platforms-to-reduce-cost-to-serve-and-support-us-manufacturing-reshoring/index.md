## Overview

U.S. manufacturers must lower cost-to-serve (CoTS) while improving the economics of reshoring production. This paper demonstrates how combining industrial IoT/edge devices, hyperscale cloud services, and customer-operations platforms with artificial intelligence (AI) can deliver measurable improvements in maintenance, quality, scheduling, energy efficiency, and service operations. We propose a brand-neutral Signal-to-Action reference architecture and a practical evaluation plan---including staggered adoption comparisons, pilot deployments with synthetic twin benchmarks, and high-frequency time-series tracking---paired with auditable CoTS accounting and a clear translation of operational gains into reshoring viability. We evaluate the framework through a discrete-event simulation study of a representative manufacturing facility ($N = 100$ replications per condition, 30-day warm-up period) across four deployment scenarios---reactive baseline, threshold-based maintenance, partial AI, and full stack---with parameters calibrated to published industry benchmarks. The simulation demonstrates a 56.8 % CoTS reduction ( $99.03 $ rightarrow$ $42.77 per order) under the modeled conditions, with all pairwise comparisons significant at $p < 0.001$. The result is an implementation-ready playbook that organizations can apply to achieve credible CoTS reduction and strengthen the case for U.S. manufacturing reshoring.
